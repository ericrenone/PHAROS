# PHAROS

## *Energy Across the Long Day — From the Aeolipile to the Quantum Geometric Engine*

---

## Abstract

The first known device to convert heat into rotation was Hero of Alexandria's *aeolipile*, a hollow sphere on bearings, vented tangentially, spun by escaping steam — a working heat engine constructed nineteen centuries before its industrial rediscovery. **PHAROS** uses this object as the conceptual lighthouse for a unified framework of energy, time, and action: every engine — classical, statistical, quantum, geometric — is, at its core, a closed loop in some space, enclosing an *area* whose value is the work extracted in one cycle. The framework formalises this thesis across two thousand years of physics, from Hero's tangential jets to the 2026 SOTA on flat-band geometric supercurrent, and proposes a single area-action principle from which thermodynamic, semiclassical, and quantum-geometric extractions of energy all descend.

---

## 1 · The aeolipile as proto-engine

Hero's device, described in his *Pneumatica* (c. 60 CE):

- A sealed boiler heated below.
- A hollow sphere mounted on a horizontal axle, fed by two pipes from the boiler.
- Two L-shaped jets exit the sphere tangentially, in opposite directions.
- Steam exits, the sphere spins.

This is a *Carnot machine in archaic dress*: heat enters as enthalpy of steam; work leaves as rotational kinetic energy; reactive force on the sphere is the *boundary integral* of momentum flux — a Shoelace-type loop integral made of escaping mass.

> **Thought experiment.** The aeolipile traces a closed curve in a thermodynamic state plane: steam enters at high $(P, T)$, exits at low $(P, T)$, the sphere returns to its initial state every revolution. The work per cycle is the *area* of this loop. Hero, without calculus, was implicitly computing $\oint P\, dV$ — a Shoelace formula evaluated by Nature, in steam.

This is the central observation of PHAROS: **a heat engine is a planimeter on a state diagram.**

## 2 · The area–action axis

Across all of physics, work and action are the *same kind of quantity* — an integral of a one-form around a closed loop, equal to the flux of its exterior derivative through any surface the loop bounds. Three landmark examples:

1. **Clausius (1865).** Reversible heat: $\delta Q_\text{rev} = T\, dS$. Work in a Carnot cycle = $\oint T\, dS$ = enclosed area in $T$–$S$ space.
2. **Bohr–Sommerfeld (1916).** Semiclassical quantisation: $\oint p\, dq = (n + \tfrac{1}{2})\hbar$. Phase-space area = quantum number.
3. **Berry (1984).** Adiabatic quantum geometry: $\gamma = \oint i \langle \psi | d\psi \rangle = \int \mathcal{F}$. Parameter-space area of the Berry curvature = geometric phase.

In all three, the key object is a *line integral around a closed curve, equal to a surface integral of the curl* — Stokes' theorem in different costumes. The Shoelace formula is the discrete prototype; the Cayley–Menger determinant is the metric realisability test for the loop's vertices; Pick-Ehrhart counting is the topological invariant of the lattice version.

PHAROS asserts:

$$\boxed{\; \text{Work / Action / Phase} \;=\; \oint \omega \;=\; \int_{\text{enclosed}} d\omega \;}$$

with $\omega$ a thermodynamic, mechanical, or quantum one-form. The aeolipile is the simplest physical realisation; the quantum geometric tensor is the most refined.

## 3 · Time as the parameter of the loop

Hero's wheel turns. Time is the parameter along the cycle, not a coordinate of the loop. This is the essential distinction:

- **Loop space:** $(P, V)$, $(T, S)$, $(p, q)$, $(\theta_1, \theta_2)$ in a Brillouin zone, $(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$ in adiabatic parameter space.
- **Time:** the *parameter* tracing the loop.

The work extracted *per cycle* is independent of how fast the loop is traversed (in the reversible limit) — it is purely the enclosed area. The *power* depends on cycle frequency. **Energy per cycle is geometric; power is dynamical.** This separation, implicit in Hero, made explicit in Carnot, formalised in Berry, is the temporal signature of PHAROS.

> **Thought experiment.** A Sommerfeld-quantised electron, a Carnot engine, a Berry-phase qubit, and Hero's aeolipile all execute closed loops in their respective state spaces. Slow each loop down by a factor of a million. The *work / action / phase per cycle is unchanged*. Now compare them when the cycle area is the same: a Carnot engine of $\oint T\, dS = \hbar/2$ extracts the same numerical work-equivalent as a Bohr–Sommerfeld orbit of one quantum, or a Berry path enclosing $\pi$ steradians on the Bloch sphere. *Numerical equality across two thousand years of physics is unlikely to be coincidence.*

## 4 · The 2026 quantum-geometric heat engine

Current SOTA flat-band research has converged on a remarkable picture: in materials with weakly dispersive bands, the supercurrent is set by the integrated quantum metric, not by group velocity. Concretely, the superfluid weight tensor

$$D^s_{\mu\nu} \;=\; D^{\text{conv}}_{\mu\nu} \;+\; D^{\text{geom}}_{\mu\nu}$$

decomposes into a conventional (band-derivative) part and a geometric (quantum-metric) part. In the flat-band limit, $D^{\text{conv}} \to 0$ and $D^{\text{geom}}$ alone supports finite supercurrent, with a topological lower bound by the Chern number squared. This is, in PHAROS terms, a *quantum aeolipile*: the loop in Brillouin zone × pairing-phase space encloses a Berry-curvature flux whose integral *is* the dissipationless current.

Recent results — including the 2026 work on $\mathcal{T}_3$-lattice tunable quasi-flat bands and quasicrystal superconductors with flux-space metric formulations — confirm that the loop need not live in momentum space at all; it lives in whatever parameter space the Hamiltonian responds to. **The aeolipile principle is parameter-space-agnostic.**

## 5 · The cosmological long day

From Hero (~60 CE) to Pick (1899) to Hund (1925) to Provost-Vallee (1980) to Berry (1984) to Törmä-Peotta-Bernevig (2022) to the 2026 flat-band metric work: roughly 1965 years of intellectual time, during which the *same area-action principle* has been recovered in ever more refined linguistic dress. PHAROS reads this not as accident but as *historical convergence to a fixed point*: the area-around-a-loop is the irreducible content of energy extraction, and successive generations have rediscovered it in their respective formal vocabularies.

> **Thought experiment.** Imagine running the long day in reverse. Begin with the 2026 quantum geometric tensor and ask: what minimal classical object encodes the same content? Strip the band index — get a single-particle Berry phase. Strip the parameter circle — get a Bohr–Sommerfeld orbit. Strip the quantum — get a Carnot cycle. Strip the engineering — get an aeolipile. *The latest theory is the oldest device wearing more rigorous clothes.* This is what convergence to a fixed point looks like when traced backwards.

## 6 · The triangle of energies

PHAROS organises energetic phenomena into a triangle:

- **Thermal vertex:** Hero, Carnot, Clausius — energy as the area of $(T, S)$ or $(P, V)$ loops.
- **Mechanical vertex:** Maupertuis, Hamilton, Bohr–Sommerfeld — action as the area of $(p, q)$ loops.
- **Geometric vertex:** Provost–Vallee, Berry, Törmä — phase and supercurrent as the area of $(\lambda_1, \lambda_2)$ or Brillouin-zone loops.

The three vertices are linked by the same Stokes' theorem on different state manifolds. The arrows between them are *Legendre transforms* (thermal ↔ mechanical) and *Wick rotations* (mechanical ↔ geometric). The triangle is closed: starting at any vertex, one can reach the others by formal transformations that preserve the loop area.

This is the *energetic counterpart* of the geometric SOMA triality: where SOMA is intrinsic / extrinsic / topological, PHAROS is thermal / mechanical / geometric. Together they form a six-vertex diagram — three geometric area-tongues × two area-action axes — covering the bulk of classical and quantum extensive physics.

## 7 · Predictions

1. **Universal cycle bound.** For any cyclic process — thermodynamic, semiclassical, or quantum-geometric — the work / action / phase per cycle is bounded above by an area in its native state space and below by a topological invariant of the loop's homotopy class. Hero's aeolipile saturates the upper bound; the Berry phase saturates the lower; flat-band supercurrents interpolate.
2. **Time-independence of yield.** In the adiabatic limit, the extracted quantity per cycle is the *enclosed area only*; cycle period drops out. Departures from this give precise non-adiabatic corrections measurable in fast-driven flat-band devices.
3. **Reverse engineering.** Any new energetic mechanism discovered in the next decade can be cast as a loop in some state manifold; the area integral of a one-form around that loop will compute its yield. PHAROS predicts this is *not* an analogy but the *only* form a self-consistent energetic mechanism can take.

## 8 · The lighthouse

A *pharos* — the lighthouse of Alexandria, a Wonder of the ancient world built in Hero's hometown — illuminates a coast: not by bringing the ship to harbour itself, but by marking, from a fixed elevated point, the geometry of safe passage. PHAROS the framework occupies the same role with respect to the long history of energy: it does not perform the calculations of any specific era, but illuminates that all of them are pinned to a single fixed point — *the area enclosed by a closed loop is the energy of the cycle* — and that every advance has been a finer optical lens on the same beacon.

## 9 · Conclusions

1. Hero's aeolipile already implements the area-action principle: work per cycle = enclosed loop area in a thermodynamic state space.
2. Carnot, Bohr–Sommerfeld, and Berry are the same principle expressed in increasingly abstract loop spaces.
3. The 2026 quantum geometric tensor and superfluid-weight programme show this principle as a topological lower bound on dissipationless current — the deepest current incarnation.
4. Time is not a coordinate of the loop but its parameter; the geometric content (work, action, phase) is *time-independent* in the adiabatic limit, while power is dynamical.
5. The historical sequence is not a chain of accidents but convergence to a fixed point of energy theory: an area, around a loop, in a state space.

The aeolipile spins; the metric integrates; the Carnot loop closes; the Berry phase accumulates; the supercurrent flows. *In each, an area is being measured.* PHAROS is the proposition that this is what energy *is*.
